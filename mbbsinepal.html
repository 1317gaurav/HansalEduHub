Duration of MBBS in Nepal

MBBS course duration in Nepal is of five years and a half years. Initial years are of theoretical classes and basic study of medical sciences and clinical study. In the last year, students are required to go through the internship program in clinics of hospitals of the college or university. Such courses also offer options for recruiting the students on the completion of the course. Students who have passed the 10+2 with the science background are entitled to apply for the MBBS admission in Nepal.
To get admission in Nepal, it is vital for the students to meet the certain stipulated criteria set by the medical universities/colleges. There will be no relaxation provided to the students in the context of eligibility. Nepal Mbbs for the students who would like to appear should meet the eligibility criteria.

Syllabus for MBBS Program in Nepal

The study pattern of Nepalese medical colleges is similar to India. The total duration of the course is 5 and 1/2 years including a 1-year internship. First two years they will teach basic medical sciences and in the next 2 and a half years, students will learn the principles and skills of clinical medicine through rotating internships.

About Nepal

Overview

Nepal is the Federal Democratic Republic and is a sovereign country which is located in the Himalayas. It is in the North part of the country, which has the tallest mountain range in the world, including that of Mount Everest. Lord Buddha’s birth, namely, Lumbini place also lies in this particular region. It is one of the most renowned pilgrimage spots in the world. Kathmandu is the capital of Nepal. Nepal is famous for its multi-ethnicity and multi-cultural background.
The total area of Nepal is 147,181 km trapezoidal shape which covers 800 Km long and 200 Km wide space. When we see the physiography of Nepal, it is divided into Himal, Pahad, and Terai. Himal is the mountain region containing snow and situated in Himalayan Range. Pahad is also mountain region but generally does not contain snow. Terai is the lowland region, also contains some hill ranges. The Terai region is formed and fed by three major Himalayan rivers.

Climate & Weather

When we talk about Climate of Nepal, it has five climatic zones. There are five seasons that Nepalese experience; summer, monsoon, autumn, winter, and spring. Nepal has very pleasant environment due to the mountainous region.
In the summer season, the maximum temperature goes 25-300C in the month of May-august. Rainfall in monsoon season goes to 40 to 55 and minimum temperature 100C. Nepalese feels 5-20average temperature in the month of January and sometimes it goes below 0

Religion

Majority of the people in Nepal follows Hinduism and believer of Lord Shiva. Studying and living in Nepal does not make you feel a strange country. Everything in Nepal is similar to India.
